This text is by its nature strange.
It's the source text for the second half of \texttt{CAS CS 320}: \textit{Concepts of Programming Languages} at Boston University, and the intended audience is ultimately students in this course.
My hope is that it might find a wider audience, but describing a more inclusive notion of \enquote{intended audience} requires some context: \texttt{CS320} is a course in the computer science cirriculum at Boston University that is semi-required.
As much as I appreciate this, and agree that everyone should know \textit{something} about how programming languages work, there is a non-negligible slice of the student population that passes through our course who don't agree with this sentiment.
And so we're put in the difficult but perennial position: \textit{how to we get students interested in a topic they're studying because they're told they have to?}
The point: the intended audience of this text is undergraduate students who don't necessarily want to do research in programming languages, but \enquote{want} to know something about how programming languages work.
There's a lot we don't cover, and although we try to maintain the rigor of some of the more serious texts in the field \cite{scott2000programming, pierce2002types, harper2016practical, ramsey2022programming}, we're not gonna to lose sleep over the less-than-complete coverage we inevitably end up with.

Another operative part of the description of our context of is \enquote{the second half of.}
We typically spend the first half of the course teaching OCaml from \href{https://cs3110.github.io/textbook/cover.html}{OCaml Programming: Correct + Efficient + Beautiful}.
In fact, this text should be viewed as an extension of that one, with the section on interpreters further fleshed out.
As such, we're greatly in debt to Michael R. Clarkson et al. who have put the time and energy into that work.

This text is based on material created by several members of the \textit{Principles of Programming and Verification (POPV)} group at Boston University, most notably Ankush Das, Robin Fu, Marco Gaboardi, Assaf Kfoury, and Hongwei Xi.
