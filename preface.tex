This text is by its nature strange.
It's the source text for the second part of \texttt{CAS CS 320}: \textit{Concepts of Programming Languages} at Boston University, and the intended audience is ultimately students in this course.
My hope is that it might find a wider audience, but describing a more inclusive notion of \enquote{intended audience} requires some context: \texttt{CS320} is a course in the computer science cirriculum at Boston University that is semi-required.
And as much as I believe that everyone should know \textit{something} about how programming languages work, there is a non-negligible portion of the student population that does \textit{not} agree with this sentiment.
So we're put in the difficult but perennial position: \textit{how do we get students interested in a topic they're studying because they're told they have to?}
The point: the intended audience of this text is undergraduate students who don't necessarily want to do research in programming languages, but \enquote{want} to know something about how programming languages work.
There's a lot we don't cover, and although we try to maintain the rigor of some of the more serious texts in the field \cite{scott2000programming, pierce2002types, harper2016practical, ramsey2022programming} we're not gonna to lose sleep over the less-than-complete coverage we inevitably end up with.

The other operative part of the description above is \enquote{the second part of}.
We typically spend the first part of the course teaching OCaml from \href{https://cs3110.github.io/textbook/cover.html}{OCaml Programming: Correct + Efficient + Beautiful}.
This text should be viewed to some degree as an extension of that one, with the section on interpreters fleshed out.
As such, we're greatly in debt to Michael R. Clarkson et al. who have put the time and energy into that amazing (and freely available) book.

This text is based on material created by several members of the \textit{Principles of Programming and Verification (POPV)} group at Boston University, most notably Ankush Das, Robin Fu, Marco Gaboardi, Assaf Kfoury, and Hongwei Xi.
